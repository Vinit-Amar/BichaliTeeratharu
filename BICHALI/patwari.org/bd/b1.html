Bhikshaalaya Darshana - 1

(Sanctum of Bhikshaalaya )

Date : 28 Jun 2006

Background

The sanctum of Bhikshaalaya (Bichchale) of Sri Appanacharya, its abundant nature’s gift and the liking of the great pontiffs are described in Bhikshaalaya (1).

Meaning                    

I feel and think that it is my and sacred duty to describe Bhikshaalaya to our devotees in Mantralaya Series. The native place of Sri Appanacharya is famous, popular and wellknown for its sacred sanctum. Mother nature’s gift is ample.Tungabhadra river is flowing with force. Bhikshaalaya people pronounce as Bichchale for their convenience. Bhikshaalaya is situated in the district of Raichur, taluka of Raichur, Karnataka (India). It is between Mumbai- Chennai Railway line. After crossing the river Tungabhadra we will find Tungabhadra railway station now called Mantralayam Road. Before crossing the river Tungabhadra we will find Bhikshaalaya a little far about five kilometers from the Railway line. Mantralaya is fifteen kilometers from Mantralayam Road. Bhikshaalaya is on the bank of Tungabhadra with full of trees and green fields, crops nod their heads with joy under the kind affection of mother nature. Tungabhadra river’s flow has added enormous beauty and importance to Bhikshaalaya. Though Bhikshaalaya is a small village, owing to the stay of divine personalities, it has attained great importance.

Bhikshaalaya is very sacred that it has given birth to Sri Appanacharya a great devotee,full of knowledge,imagination, creativity,a great patriot and the follower of Sri Madacharya.Here there is a stone platform under the thick shadow of Vatavraksha, very adjacent to the river Tungabhadra. Devotees call this stone platform as JAPADA KATTE. The sanctity and the profound silence are quite pleasing to the divine personalities. Our Mulabagilu Sri Sripadarajaru simply gave his heart to the cool and comfort sanctum, stayed here for some days and spent in Japa-Tapa, Dhyaana-Aahneeka. Sri Sripadarajaru has done the pratishthhaapana of Sri UgraNarasimhaDevaru killing HiranyaKashyapu on his laps with the nails, thus denoted the arrival of Bhakta Prahlada Avataari Sri Raghavendrateertharu on this calm and quiet, cool and comfort bank of Tungabhadra. Sri Sripadarajaru did Paathha-Pravachana sitting on this JAPADA KATTE. How sacred and what an amount of Punya is there in Bhikshaalaya !.

The JAPADA KATTE as already said is very close to the river, under the immense thick cool shadow of Vatavraksha. Tungabhadra is flowing with force through several rocky stones of different sizes making different noise. The Tungabhadra’s flowing is the only companion to the residents of Bichchale. Satvikas feel and think that this sound is nothing but the Omkaara of Sri Harisarvottama and Vayujeevottama.Thus the JAPADA KATTE is famous and popular for its Sanctity and welcome the Sadhu-Santas-Rishi-Munis. Sri Vyasarajaru has done the Pratishthhaapana of PraanaDevaru in his own style using the charcoal for the sketch. Like this more than 732 PraanaDevaru are installed by Sri Vyasarajaru through out the bank of Tungabhadra only. It is quite heartening to note the unique way of propagation of VayuJeevottama Tatva by Sri Vyasarayaru. Sri Jitamitraru also spent some days on this JAPADA KATTE and has done the Pratishthhaapana of Shesha Devaru on the Shila very beautifully The total scene of Tungabhadra river is  encuraging, rejoicing ,rhythmic under the mother nature’s glorified greenery at the JAPADA KATTE.

The geographical features are quite soothing to the saints-satvikas and thus Bhikshaalaya has attained an important position by our great Sri Appanacharya and Mantralaya  Rayaru.The stay of Mulabagilu Sripadarajaru, Sri Vyasarajaru, Sri Jitamitraru, Sri Raghavendrateertharu and Sri Appanacharya have engraved Bhakti-Gnyaana-Vairaagya on the  rocks of Bhikshaalaya on the bank of Tungabhadra and sowed in the red soil of Bhikshaalaya. By road while going to Mantralaya at the left we find Panchamukhi that is Gaanadhaalu  Panchamukhi, at the right Bhikshaalaya enrouting Gille Sugur Camp and Yele Bichchale. Panchamukhi and Bhikshaalaya are to the left and right of Mantralaya very dear to our Sri Madacharya and welcome the devotees.The greatness of Bhikshaalaya will be continued  in the next Mantralaya Series
